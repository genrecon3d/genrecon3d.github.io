Introduction

GenRecon3D consolidates emerging directions at the intersection of generative modeling and geometric reconstruction. The workshop targets faithful recovery of underlying 3D and potentially 4D geometry from incomplete observations. Unlike approaches prioritizing visual plausibility, we emphasize metric accuracy, physical correctness, and principled uncertainty reasoning.

The program features invited talks, oral and poster presentations, and community discussions on evaluation protocols and robust benchmarks that assess geometric and semantic accuracy beyond visible surfaces.

Broader impacts and ethics

Progress in generative reconstruction has implications for robotics, autonomous systems, digital twins, and AR/VR, where accurate scene reconstruction is essential under incomplete sensing. The workshop prioritizes truthful recovery of real environments, evaluation beyond visible surfaces, and uncertainty-aware inference.

Social and ethical considerations include privacy and surveillance risks, and potential biases from training data. We commit to screening submissions for ethical misconduct and misuse, encouraging transparency and bias mitigation, and including an ethics-focused segment in the panel discussion.
